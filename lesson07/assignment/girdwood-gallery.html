First & Last Freeze/Frost dates

You are almost guaranteed that you will not get frost in Anchorage from May 4th through October 4th.

Girdwood tends to be 2-3 weeks behind Anchorage. With this in mind, I suggest waiting till June 1st to plant new plants.

As you wait for a safe frost free date, you can harden your plants off to the sun and weather by setting them outside around 8-9am and then bring them back in around 7-8pm at night till June 1st when you plant them outside.

Alpine Forget-Me-Not

Designated state flower of Alaska! You'll find this flower in moist mountainous areas on wooded slopes and grassy meadows. It's growing season is super short generating flowers between June and September.

Fireweed

Fireweed is a tall showy wildflower that grows from sea level to the subalpine zone. A colorful sight in many parts of the country, fireweed thrives in open meadows, along streams, roadsides, and forest edges.

Girdwood Gardening Spring Chores

Sprinkle river sand (dark) or ashes over the snow covering your garden in April to accelerate it's removal. The dark color absorbs sun & heat. Careful with ashes though, make sure they match the type of ash with the type of plant.
